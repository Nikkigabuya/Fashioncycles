Fashion
...is the second dirtiest industry in the world.
Right next to oil companies.
7 trillion liters of water are consumed on an annual basis in order to meet global textile demands for more than 7 million people in the US for 1 year.
The dyes used run off into the river and not only are toxic to the environment,

but to the people who still have to use these rivers for water

132 million tons of coal is consumed from garment factories on an annual basis
The world now consumes about 80 billion new pieces of clothing every year.
This is 400% more than the amount we consumed just two decades ago.
90% of clothes donated to charities actually get sold to developing countries or end up filling up landfills.
Second-hand clothing competes with local businesses and devastates their economies.
\
The average American now generates 82 lbs. of textile waste each year.
It takes 200 years for polyester to break down
Assuming infinite resources in a finite world is not a sustainable model.
